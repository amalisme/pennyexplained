What is Debt?

Credit & Debt 5 min read

Debt is money you owe to someone else. When you borrow money and haven't paid it back yet, you're in debt.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

Debt happens when you receive something of value now (money, goods, or services) and agree to pay for it later. The person or company you owe is called a creditor, and they extend credit to you.

How Debt Works

The basic debt process:

  1. You borrow money or buy on credit
  2. You owe the original amount (principal)
  3. You usually also owe interest (the cost of borrowing)
  4. You make payments until the debt is fully repaid

Example: You buy a $20,000 car with a loan. You now have $20,000 in debt. Each month, you make payments that cover part of the $20,000 plus interest until the car is paid off.

Common Types of Debt

Credit Card Debt

Money owed on credit cards. This is revolving debt - you can borrow, repay, and borrow again up to your limit.

  • Usually has high interest rates (15-25%)
  • Monthly minimum payments required
  • Can take years to pay off if you only pay minimums

High credit utilization on credit cards can hurt your credit score and make the debt harder to manage.

Student Loans

Money borrowed to pay for education. Payments often start after graduation.

  • Federal loans usually have lower rates
  • Private loans can have higher rates
  • Repayment can take 10-30 years

Auto Loans

Money borrowed to buy a vehicle.

  • Usually 3-7 year terms
  • The car serves as collateral (can be repossessed if you don't pay)
  • Interest rates vary based on credit score

Mortgages

Loans to buy real estate (homes).

  • Usually 15-30 year terms
  • The house is collateral
  • Generally lower interest rates than other debt

Personal Loans

Borrowed money for various purposes.

  • Fixed payment amounts
  • Terms typically 2-7 years
  • Can be secured (with collateral) or unsecured

Secured vs Unsecured Debt

Secured debt: Backed by collateral (an asset the lender can take if you don't pay).

  • Mortgages (house is collateral)
  • Auto loans (car is collateral)
  • Usually has lower interest rates

Unsecured debt: Not backed by collateral.

  • Credit cards
  • Most personal loans
  • Student loans
  • Usually has higher interest rates (more risk for lender)

Why People Have Debt

Major purchases: Few people can buy a house or car with cash. Debt makes these purchases possible.

Education: Investing in education through student loans can lead to higher earning potential.

Emergencies: Unexpected medical bills or car repairs sometimes require borrowing.

Convenience: Credit cards offer convenience and rewards, though carrying balances leads to debt.

The Cost of Debt

Debt costs money through interest. The longer you carry debt, the more interest you pay.

Example: A $5,000 credit card balance at 18% APR, paying $150/month:

  • Time to pay off: 47 months (almost 4 years)
  • Total interest paid: $2,000+
  • That $5,000 purchase actually cost you $7,000

Understanding the difference between good debt and bad debt helps you make smarter borrowing decisions and avoid costly mistakes.

Key Takeaways

  • Understanding credit and debt helps you make better financial decisions
  • Your credit score significantly impacts your financial opportunities
  • Managing credit responsibly saves you thousands in interest over time
  • Knowledge is power when it comes to credit and borrowing
