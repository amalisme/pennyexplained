What is Compound Interest?

Money Terms 6 min read

Compound interest is earning interest on your interest. It's what makes money grow exponentially over time and is the key to building long-term wealth.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

Compound Interest Definition: Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal amount and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest (calculated only on principal), compound interest allows your money to grow exponentially because you earn "interest on interest." It's often called the eighth wonder of the world for its wealth-building power.

Simple vs Compound Interest

Aspect Simple Interest Compound Interest
Calculated on Principal only Principal + accumulated interest
Growth pattern Linear (same each period) Exponential (accelerating)
Total return Lower Higher (often significantly)

How Compound Interest Works

Example: $1,000 at 5% annual interest

Simple Interest:

  • Year 1: $1,000 × 5% = $50 → Balance: $1,050
  • Year 2: $1,000 × 5% = $50 → Balance: $1,100
  • Year 3: $1,000 × 5% = $50 → Balance: $1,150
  • Year 10: $1,000 × 5% = $50 → Balance: $1,500

Compound Interest:

  • Year 1: $1,000 × 5% = $50 → Balance: $1,050
  • Year 2: $1,050 × 5% = $52.50 → Balance: $1,102.50
  • Year 3: $1,102.50 × 5% = $55.13 → Balance: $1,157.63
  • Year 10: Balance: $1,628.89

Difference after 10 years: $128.89 (compound earned 25% more)

The Power of Time

$10,000 invested at 7% annual return (compounded):

Years Balance Total Earned Multiple of Original
0 $10,000 $0 1.0x
10 $19,672 $9,672 2.0x
20 $38,697 $28,697 3.9x
30 $76,123 $66,123 7.6x
40 $149,745 $139,745 15.0x

Key insight: Money doesn't just double - it more than doubles, then quadruples, then keeps accelerating. This is the exponential power of compounding.

Compounding Frequency Matters

$10,000 at 5% annual interest for 10 years:

  • Annually: $16,289 (compounds once per year)
  • Quarterly: $16,436 (compounds 4 times per year)
  • Monthly: $16,470 (compounds 12 times per year)
  • Daily: $16,487 (compounds 365 times per year)

Difference: Daily compounding earns $198 more than annual compounding. The more frequently interest compounds, the more you earn.

The Rule of 72

Rule of 72 Formula: 72 ÷ Interest Rate = Years to Double

Examples:

  • At 6% annual return: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
  • At 8% annual return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double
  • At 10% annual return: 72 ÷ 10 = 7.2 years to double

Practical use: Quickly estimate how long until your money doubles at a given rate.

Real-World Compound Interest

Retirement Savings

Starting at age 25, saving $300/month at 7% return:

  • Total contributions by age 65: $144,000
  • Value at age 65: $719,073
  • Interest earned: $575,073 (4x your contributions)

Starting at age 35 (same $300/month, same 7%):

  • Total contributions by age 65: $108,000
  • Value at age 65: $339,849
  • Interest earned: $231,849

Cost of waiting 10 years: $379,224 less at retirement

Debt (Compound Interest Working Against You)

$5,000 credit card balance at 18% APR, paying minimums only:

  • Monthly minimum: ~$100
  • Years to pay off: 30+ years
  • Total interest paid: $12,000+
  • Total cost: $17,000+ for a $5,000 debt

Compound interest on debt works against you - you pay interest on accumulated interest, making debt grow exponentially if not paid down.

Maximizing Compound Interest

Strategies to make compounding work for you:

  1. Start early: Even 5-10 years makes a massive difference
  2. Invest regularly: Monthly contributions compound faster than lump sums
  3. Reinvest earnings: Don't withdraw interest/dividends - let them compound
  4. Maximize return rate: Higher rates accelerate compounding significantly
  5. Choose frequent compounding: Daily > Monthly > Quarterly > Annually
  6. Minimize fees: Fees reduce returns, which reduces compounding
  7. Stay invested long-term: Compounding accelerates with time

Impact example: Starting retirement savings at 25 vs. 35 with identical monthly contributions can result in 2-3x more money at retirement due to the extra 10 years of compounding.
