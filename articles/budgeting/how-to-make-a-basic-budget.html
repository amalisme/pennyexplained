How to Make a Basic Budget

Budgeting 8 min read

Creating your first budget is easier than you think. Follow these simple steps to take control of your money.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

Making a budget doesn't require complicated software or math skills. You just need to know how much you earn and where you spend it.

Step 1: Calculate Your Monthly Income

Start by adding up all money you receive each month:

  • Paycheck (after taxes - your take-home pay)
  • Side hustle income
  • Any other regular income

Example: Your paycheck is $2,500 twice a month = $5,000 total monthly income.

Step 2: List All Your Expenses

Write down everything you spend money on. Look at last month's bank statement to help you remember:

Fixed expenses (same every month):

  • Rent or mortgage
  • Car payment
  • Insurance
  • Loan payments

Variable expenses (change each month):

  • Groceries
  • Gas
  • Utilities
  • Entertainment
  • Dining out

Step 3: Categorize Your Spending

Group your expenses into categories. Common categories include:

  • Housing (rent, utilities, maintenance)
  • Transportation (car payment, gas, insurance)
  • Food (groceries, dining out)
  • Personal (clothing, phone, subscriptions)
  • Savings (emergency fund, goals)
  • Debt payments

Step 4: Set Spending Limits

Decide how much to allocate to each category based on your income:

Example budget for $5,000 monthly income:

  • Housing: $1,500 (30%)
  • Transportation: $750 (15%)
  • Food: $600 (12%)
  • Utilities: $200 (4%)
  • Insurance: $300 (6%)
  • Personal/Entertainment: $500 (10%)
  • Debt payments: $400 (8%)
  • Savings: $750 (15%)
  • Total: $5,000

Step 5: Track Your Spending

As you go through the month, track what you actually spend in each category. You can use:

  • A notebook
  • A spreadsheet
  • A budgeting app
  • Your bank's online tools

Step 6: Adjust as Needed

Your first budget won't be perfect. That's okay! After a month, review what worked and what didn't. Adjust your categories and amounts until it fits your real life.

If you consistently overspend in groceries but underspend in entertainment, move money between those categories. The goal is a budget that works for you.

Key Takeaways

  • Understanding budgeting fundamentals helps you take control of your money
  • Start simple and adjust as you learn what works for you
  • Consistency matters more than perfection
  • A good budget balances saving goals with enjoying life
