Monthly Budget Example

Budgeting 5 min read

Looking at a real budget example helps you understand how to create your own. Here's a realistic monthly budget with actual numbers.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

This example shows a monthly budget for someone earning $4,000 per month after taxes. Your numbers will be different, but the structure works the same way.

Sample Monthly Budget: $4,000 Income

Income

  • Monthly take-home pay: $4,000

Housing & Utilities (32.5%)

  • Rent: $1,100
  • Electricity: $80
  • Water: $40
  • Internet: $60
  • Subtotal: $1,300

Transportation (17.5%)

  • Car payment: $350
  • Car insurance: $150
  • Gas: $200
  • Maintenance fund: $0
  • Subtotal: $700

Food (15%)

  • Groceries: $450
  • Dining out: $150
  • Subtotal: $600

Personal & Healthcare (10%)

  • Health insurance: $200
  • Phone bill: $60
  • Gym membership: $40
  • Personal care: $50
  • Clothing: $50
  • Subtotal: $400

Entertainment & Misc (7.5%)

  • Streaming services: $40
  • Entertainment/hobbies: $160
  • Gifts: $100
  • Subtotal: $300

Debt & Savings (17.5%)

  • Student loan payment: $200
  • Emergency fund: $300
  • Retirement savings: $200
  • Subtotal: $700

Total Budget

Total Monthly Expenses: $4,000
Income: $4,000
Remaining: $0 ✓ Balanced budget!

Notes About This Budget

This example follows rough percentages: about 30% housing, 15% transportation, 15% food, and 15% savings. These are guidelines, not rules. Your situation may require different percentages.

Notice how every dollar has a purpose. When income equals expenses exactly, that's a "zero-based budget" - all money is allocated.

Key Takeaways

  • Understanding budgeting fundamentals helps you take control of your money
  • Start simple and adjust as you learn what works for you
  • Consistency matters more than perfection
  • A good budget balances saving goals with enjoying life
