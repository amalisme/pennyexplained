What is Overdraft?

Banking 6 min read

Overdraft occurs when you spend more money than you have in your account. Understanding how it works helps you avoid expensive fees.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

An overdraft happens when you try to spend more money than your account balance. The bank may cover the transaction and charge you a fee.

How Overdraft Works

If you have $50 in your account and spend $75, you've overdrafted by $25. The bank charges an overdraft fee (typically $25-35) on top of the negative balance.

Overdraft Protection

Some banks offer overdraft protection, which links your checking to savings or credit card to prevent overdrafts. This usually has a smaller fee ($10-12).

How to Avoid Overdrafts

  • Track your balance regularly
  • Set up low balance alerts
  • Keep a buffer in your account

Key Takeaways

  • Understanding banking fees and services helps you make better financial decisions
  • Most fees are avoidable with proper account management
  • Read your account terms carefully to understand charges
  • Choose banks and accounts that match your needs
