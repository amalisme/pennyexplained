How FDIC Insurance Works

Banking 6 min read

FDIC insurance protects your bank deposits if your bank fails. Understanding this protection gives you confidence in the banking system.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

The FDIC (Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation) is a government agency that insures bank deposits up to $250,000 per depositor, per bank.

What FDIC Insurance Covers

If your bank fails, the FDIC guarantees you'll get your money back up to $250,000. This includes checking accounts, savings accounts, CDs, and money market accounts.

What It Doesn't Cover

FDIC insurance doesn't cover investments like stocks, bonds, mutual funds, or cryptocurrency. It only covers deposit accounts at FDIC-insured banks.

How It Protects You

When a bank fails, the FDIC either finds another bank to take over your accounts or sends you a check for your balance (up to $250,000). Most people get their money within days.

All major banks are FDIC insured, which means your deposits are safe even if the bank goes out of business.
