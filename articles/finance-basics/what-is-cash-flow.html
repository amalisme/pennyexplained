What is Cash Flow?

Finance Basics 6 min read

Cash flow is the movement of money into and out of your finances. Positive cash flow means you have more coming in than going out.

Advertisement - Leaderboard (728x90)

Cash flow is simply money in versus money out. When more money comes in than goes out, you have positive cash flow. When more goes out than comes in, you have negative cash flow.

How Cash Flow Works

Cash inflow: Money coming to you (your income)

  • Paychecks
  • Side hustle income
  • Tax refunds
  • Gifts
  • Investment returns

Cash outflow: Money leaving your control

  • Rent or mortgage
  • Bills and utilities
  • Groceries
  • Debt payments
  • Entertainment
  • Savings (yes, this is outflow from spending money)

Net cash flow = Inflow - Outflow

This is essentially your net income minus your expenses.

Positive vs Negative Cash Flow

Positive Cash Flow

More money comes in than goes out

Example:

  • Monthly income: $4,000
  • Monthly expenses: $3,400
  • Net cash flow: +$600

What this means: You have $600 left over to save, invest, or spend. You're building financial stability.

Negative Cash Flow

More money goes out than comes in

Example:

  • Monthly income: $4,000
  • Monthly expenses: $4,300
  • Net cash flow: -$300

What this means: You're short $300 each month. This leads to debt, drained savings, or financial stress.

Why Cash Flow Matters

Shows financial health: You can have a good salary but still struggle if expenses are too high. Cash flow reveals if you're living within your means.

Predicts problems: Negative cash flow isn't sustainable. Eventually, you run out of savings or accumulate debt.

Enables goals: Positive cash flow creates the surplus needed to save for emergencies, pay off debt, or invest.

Highlights timing: Even with positive monthly cash flow, you can have cash flow problems if bills are due before paychecks arrive.

Cash Flow Timing

When money comes and goes matters as much as the total amounts.

Timing problem example:

  • You're paid on the 15th: $2,000
  • Rent due on the 1st: $1,200
  • Other bills throughout month: $600

On paper, you have $200 positive cash flow. But if rent is due before your paycheck, you'll overdraft or be late on rent despite earning enough monthly.

Solution: Build a buffer of one month's expenses in checking so bill timing doesn't matter. This is separate from your emergency fund.

Improving Cash Flow

Increase Income (Cash Inflow)

  • Ask for a raise
  • Get a better-paying job
  • Start a side hustle
  • Sell unused items
  • Work overtime (if available)

Decrease Expenses (Cash Outflow)

  • Cut unnecessary subscriptions
  • Reduce dining out
  • Shop for better insurance rates
  • Downsize housing or get a roommate
  • Use public transportation or carpool

Optimize Timing

  • Align bills with paycheck dates when possible
  • Build a buffer in checking
  • Use credit cards strategically (pay off monthly) to smooth timing

Tracking Your Cash Flow

Simple monthly tracking:

  1. Write down all income received this month
  2. Write down all expenses paid this month
  3. Subtract expenses from income
  4. Positive = good, negative = problem

Example tracking:

January Cash Flow:

  • Income: $3,800 (two paychecks)
  • Expenses: $3,450
  • Net: +$350

February Cash Flow:

  • Income: $3,800
  • Expenses: $4,100 (car repair)
  • Net: -$300

Over two months: +$50 net, but February was tight. This shows why emergency funds matter.

Cash Flow vs Budget

Budget: Your plan for money before the month begins

Cash flow: What actually happened with your money

You can have a perfect budget but poor cash flow if you don't stick to the budget. Cash flow is the reality check on your budget.

Warning Signs of Cash Flow Problems

  • Using credit cards to pay for regular expenses
  • Frequently overdrafting your checking account
  • Paying bills late because money isn't there
  • Unable to save anything month to month
  • Borrowing from friends or family regularly
  • Paying minimum payments on everything

If you see these signs, you have a cash flow problem that needs immediate attention.
